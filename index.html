Remembering Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam: A Visionary Leader and People's President

  • Early Life and Education: Abdul Kalam's early life was marked by simplicity and hard work. Growing up in a middle-class Muslim family, he imbibed values of discipline and perseverance from his parents. Despite facing financial constraints, Kalam excelled in academics and earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Scientific Achievements: Dr. Kalam's journey in the field of science and technology began at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). His contribution to the successful development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and his pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests showcased his brilliance and leadership in the scientific community.
  • Missile Man of India: The title "Missile Man of India" was bestowed upon Kalam for his outstanding work in the development of ballistic missile technology. Under his leadership, India successfully conducted several crucial missile tests, including the Agni and Prithvi series. His vision for a self-reliant and technologically advanced India became the driving force behind these accomplishments.
  • ACADEMIC BACKGROUND
  • Presidency and People's President: In 2002, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his tenure, he continued to inspire the nation with his simplicity, approachability, and commitment to education. He dedicated a significant portion of his presidency to interacting with students, encouraging them to dream big and work towards a brighter future.
  • 2015 : Dies at the age of 83
